    E Side Calculation: Floor Plate Breakdown by Setback
    Podium Parking Allocation: Podium parking floors are allocated sequentially across the three setback zones in accordance with DCR 1991 / DCPR regulations. The 0–24 m zone (6 m setback) is utilised first; any residual parking requirement beyond the capacity of that zone is accommodated in the 24–37.5 m zone (9 m setback), and thereafter in the zone above 37.5 m (12 m setback). The applicable floor plate of each respective zone is used for its parking allocation. The height available within the 6 m and 9 m setback zones is fixed at 24 m and 13.5 m respectively by regulation; within the 12 m setback zone, the available height is the difference between the Maximum Permissible Height and 37.5 m.

    Office Floor Determination: The number of permissible office floors in each zone is governed by the Permissible BUA established in Section B, and not solely by the maximum permissible height. The columns below present, for reference, the maximum number of floors each zone could physically accommodate at full height ("Floors to be Achieved" / "Achievable BUA"). The operative computation distributes the Permissible BUA across zones in sequence — 6 m zone first, then 9 m, then 12 m — with the office floor count in each zone determined by the BUA allotment for that zone. The building height is accordingly optimised to consume only the sanctioned FSI entitlement without exceeding the permissible BUA limit.

    ✦Feasibility Remark— —
    ▶ Sections G & H — Revised Calculation (9 m Side-Setback Band, Height Fixed at 37.5 m) —
    1. Why Reading A (Sections A–F) may fail: At the plot's full permissible height, the calculation above achieves less BUA than what is permissible. BUA can legally be built up to the permissible ceiling but never below it, so an outcome under 100% is non-compliant and cannot be adopted as-is.
    2. Revised method — height & front margin: The proposal caps building height at 37.5 m (the height at which the 9 m side-setback floor-plate band maxes out) instead of the full plot-permissible height. Under DCR Reg. 31(1), height is capped at 1.5 × (road width + front open space); at a fixed 37.5 m height this is inverted to solve for the front margin: Front Margin = (Height ÷ 1.5) − Road Width. This gives a smaller, DCR-compliant front setback than the standard 12 m, which increases the usable floor length.
    3. Massing, floor count & FSI: With a 9 m setback on all sides (front margin above + 9 m sides), the floor plate = (Width − 2×9) × (Length − Front Margin − 9). Floors = INT(37.5 ÷ 3.3) floor-to-floor height. Gross volume = Floors × Floor Plate; countable BUA is taken at 70% efficiency (chowks/shafts/common areas excluded). FSI = Countable BUA ÷ Plot Area — the FSI the revised massing actually consumes, independent of the plot's permissible FSI ceiling.
    4. Parking strategy: Podium parking is avoided wherever possible; basement usage is maximised to 3 floors (at 1.5 m basement setback) to absorb the bulk of the parking demand below grade. Parking demand uses the same rates as Section D (37.5 Sqm/space up to 1,500 Sqm BUA, 75 Sqm/space beyond, +10% visitor, 35 Sqm built area per car); only the residual demand not met by the 3 basement floors is pushed up into podium floors on the upper (9 m-setback) floor plate.
    5. Two scales — Sections G & H: The revised calculation is presented at two scales using the same method: Section G applies it to a single subplot in isolation; Section H applies it to the combined/overall plot, for plots formed of two subplots read both individually and jointly.
    6. Constants used: Capped building height = 37.5 m  •  Office floor-to-floor height = 3.3 m  •  Side setback = 9 m (both sides)  •  DCR height/setback divisor = 1.5 (Reg. 31(1))  •  BUA efficiency = 70% of gross volume  •  Basement floors = 3 @ 1.5 m setback  •  Parking: 37.5 Sqm/space up to 1,500 Sqm BUA, 75 Sqm/space beyond, +10% visitor allowance, 35 Sqm built area per car space.
    7. Variables (inputs → derived): Inputs: subplot width, subplot length, plot/combined area, road width. Derived: front margin, floor plate area, gross volume, countable (70%) BUA, FSI, number of floors, basement floor plate, total parking demand, and residual podium parking.

    Regulatory & Methodological Notes
    1. Basement Setback: The minimum setback of the basement from the plot boundary is 1.5 m in accordance with Mumbai DCR 1991. Where tree-cutting along the periphery is involved, this setback increases to 3.0 m as stipulated under the applicable regulations.
    2. Parking Standards: Parking requirements are computed in accordance with DCR 1991 / DCPR norms at the following rates: 1 car space per 37.5 Sqm of BUA up to 1,500 Sqm; 1 car space per 75 Sqm for BUA in excess of 1,500 Sqm; with an additional 10% of the total computed parking provided for visitor use.
    3. Rounding Convention: All floor counts and parking-space counts are rounded up to the nearest whole number. Fractional floors or fractional parking spaces are not permissible in practice.
    4. Input Fields: Fields highlighted in gold denote plot-specific inputs to be entered by the user. Fields displayed in green are computed automatically from the entered values and the applicable regulatory parameters.
